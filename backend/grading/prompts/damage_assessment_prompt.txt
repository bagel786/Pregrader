CREASE_DEPTH: Severity of visible creases/folds/wrinkles
- "none": Absolutely no visible crease, fold, wrinkle, or distortion
  Visual anchor: The card surface is completely flat; no lines, ridges, or distortions visible from any angle.
- "hairline": Very faint crease barely visible, minimal surface impact
  Visual anchor: A very faint surface stress mark only visible at glancing angles; the surface is not broken and the card geometry is undisturbed.
- "moderate": Clear crease visible with some surface distortion
  Visual anchor: A clearly visible crease line where the card was bent — obvious without magnification, but card edges are still relatively straight.
- "heavy": OBVIOUS crease(s) - surface is visibly broken/bent/folded, card geometry clearly distorted
  Visual anchor: The card geometry is visibly distorted; the fold is deep enough to cast a shadow and the card no longer lies flat.

RULE: If the card shows ANY visible fold, crease line, or bent area → AT MINIMUM "hairline". If the crease is obvious and distorts the card → "heavy".
Err on the side of severity.

WHITENING_COVERAGE: Extent of visible whitening/wear (physical wear exposing white card stock)
- "none": No whitening visible at all — edges and corners retain full ink/colour

  Affects roughly 5–20% of the card border area. Clearly visible without magnification.
  Visual anchor: Two opposite corners show whitening, or one entire edge has consistent white wear visible end-to-end. Obvious without magnification.
- "extensive": Widespread whitening across 3+ locations, multiple full edges, or heavy overall border wear.
  Affects > 20% of the card border area. Unmistakable at a glance.
  Visual anchor: 3+ corners show white, or multiple full edges are worn — white visible everywhere along the border at a glance.
